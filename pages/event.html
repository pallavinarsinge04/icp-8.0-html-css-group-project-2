Events and Shows

"We host grand celebrations to make everyone feel at home. We give new sarees and bangles to all the women at the NGO. We also have many Ganapati idols and pujas, and take the residents, including those with disabilities, to visit pandals close to our organisation. Most people here come from an underprivileged background or have been abandoned. This is their chance at happiness." Utsav Life, another old-age residential home in the city with hi-tech facilities, in-house medical staff, chefs and several other perks, involves its golden-aged residents in the press of transforming the residence into a festive home with various events. "We have planned several programmes such as a dhol tasha performance for a day, aartis and special festive meals for the residents. We also conduct workshops where the residents design and make d√©cor items. We use these items to deck up the place. All these moments make the residents feel at home with us,"

"Some old age homes are doing a good job, but many do not have activities to keep them engaged or mentally healthy. In such cases, many people end up with depression, which leads to dementia. We plan to conduct a survey to understand their state of mind. The state of abandoned, underprivileged people is definitely better after organisations take them in," said Anjali Raje, the executive director of International Longevity Centre, India, a not-for-profit organisation working for the benefit of population ageing.
